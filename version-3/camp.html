Quick Information:

  • Meeting: 8:30am outside the school hall
  • Returning: 3:30pm the next day
  • Gear list here
  • Wear non-uniform clothing suitable for outdoor activities
  • Remember packed lunch for both days

Summary:

Students will spend two days at camp Wainui, sleeping one night in tents with their friends. On the first day, they will rotate through a series of fun activities at the campsite as small groups. These activities are:

  • Raft building
  • Emergency bivvy making
  • Search and rescue

On the second day, they will take a day pack and walk along the Orongorongo track. This is a lovely walk in the Remutaka valley, and has lots of beautiful native bush. Along the way, students will learn about the native plants around them, and improve their map-reading skills.

At the end of the track is a braided river with lovely views of the hills around it. When students reach it, they will learn to make fires on the riverbed, and maybe toast some marshmallows. If the river is not too high, they will also learn about river-crossing safely. After lunch, they will walk back along the track and return to camp.

Details:

Students meet with their form class in front of the school hall at 8:30am. They will then take a bus to camp. After pitching their tents, they will begin the day’s activities. Dinner will be provided, which is chili mince with pasta, and ice cream for dessert. After dinner, there will be free time until lights out at 9:30pm. After lights out, students must remain in their own tents.

On the second day, they will be provided with toast and cereal for breakfast, before taking down their tents and setting off on the walk with their day packs. After they return, they will catch a bus back to school, where they will be released around 3:30pm.

The campsite has toilets, a few showers, and a hall which we will use for dining. Use of showers is strongly discouraged, as 260 people would take a long time to shower, and we are only there for one night. There is very limited cell phone coverage, so if you need to contact your child in an emergency, you may contact the school during school hours, or Camp Wainui after hours. Phone numbers available on the Contact page.

Gear List:

All items should be labelled with the student’s name and form class in case they are lost, as there will be a lot of people on site. Please leave music speakers at home. If you are able to provide a tent, that would be great.

Main bag, containing:

  • Sleeping bag
  • Roll mat or inflatable mattress (highly recommended)
  • Full change of clothes (warm & suitable for activities)
  • Pyjamas
  • Warm hat
  • Small towel
  • Togs (if you wish to go in the lagoon for raft building & kayaking)
  • Toothbrush + toothpaste
  • Sanitary items

Optional Items:

  • Inflatable pillow or pillow case (case can be stuffed with clothing to act as a pillow)
  • Sports ball or playing cards etc. for evening entertainment
  • Slides/jandals for evening wear
  • Insect repellent

Day pack must be separate, containing:

  • Packed lunches for 2 days
  • Water bottle (at least 1 litre)
  • Sun hat
  • Sun block
  • Warm garment (e.g. jersey, thermal)
  • Rain jacket
  • Any personal medication
